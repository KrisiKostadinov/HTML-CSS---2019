class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology>

The name Bulgaria is derived from the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars, a tribe of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic origin that founded the country. Their name is not completely understood and difficult to trace back earlier than the 4th century AD,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden1992103%E2%80%93104-10">[7] but it is possibly derived from the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Turkic" class="mw-redirect" title="Proto-Turkic">Proto-Turkic word bulģha ("to mix", "shake", "stir") and its derivative bulgak ("revolt", "disorder").="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-11">[8] The meaning may be further extended to "rebel", "incite" or "produce a state of disorder", i.e. the "disturbers".="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChen201297-12">[9]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-13">[10]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGolden1992104-14">[11] Ethnic groups in ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_Asia" title="Inner Asia">Inner Asia with phonologically similar names were frequently described in similar terms: during the 4th century, the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jie_people" title="Jie people">Buluoji, a component of the "="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Barbarians" title="Five Barbarians">Five Barbarian" groups in Ancient China, were portrayed as both a "mixed race" and "troublemakers".="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChen201292%E2%80%9395,_97-15">[12]

Not long after the Slavic incursion, ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moesia" title="Moesia">Moesia was once again invaded, this time by the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars under ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khan_(title)" title="Khan (title)">Khan ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asparuh_of_Bulgaria" title="Asparuh of Bulgaria">Asparukh.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-44">[41] Their horde was a remnant of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Great_Bulgaria" title="Old Great Bulgaria">Old Great Bulgaria, an extinct tribal confederacy situated north of the Black Sea in what is now Ukraine. Succeeding rulers strengthened the Bulgarian state throughout the 8th and 9th centuries. ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krum_of_Bulgaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Krum of Bulgaria">Krum introduced a written code of law="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-46">[43] and checked a major Byzantine incursion at the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Pliska" title="Battle of Pliska">Battle of Pliska, in which Byzantine emperor ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicephorus_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicephorus I">Nicephorus I was killed.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-47">[44] ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boris_I_of_Bulgaria" title="Boris I of Bulgaria">Boris I abolished paganism in favour of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Christianity in 864. After Simeon's death, Bulgaria was weakened by wars with ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magyars" class="mw-redirect" title="Magyars">Magyars and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pechenegs" title="Pechenegs">Pechenegs and the spread of the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bogomilism" title="Bogomilism">Bogomil heresy.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-EB-50">[47]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-52">[49] Preslav was seized by the Byzantine army in 971 after consecutive ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kievan_Rus%27" title="Kievan Rus'">Rus' and Byzantine invasions.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-EB-50">[47] The empire briefly recovered from the attacks under ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_of_Bulgaria" title="Samuel of Bulgaria">Samuil,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-53">[50] but this ended when Byzantine emperor ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basil_II" title="Basil II">Basil II defeated the Bulgarian army at ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Kleidion" title="Battle of Kleidion">Klyuch in 1014. Samuil died shortly after the battle,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-54">[51] and by 1018 the Byzantines ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_conquest_of_Bulgaria" title="Byzantine conquest of Bulgaria">had conquered the First Bulgarian Empire.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrampton19874-55">[52]

The Ottomans were employed as mercenaries by the Byzantines in the 1340s but later became invaders in their own right.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Ottoman_rule-62">[59] Sultan ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murad_I" title="Murad I">Murad I took ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adrianople" class="mw-redirect" title="Adrianople">Adrianople from the Byzantines in 1362; ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia fell in 1382, followed by ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shumen" title="Shumen">Shumen in 1388.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Ottoman_rule-62">[59] The Ottomans completed their conquest of Bulgarian lands in 1393 when Tarnovo was sacked after a three-month siege and the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Nicopolis" title="Battle of Nicopolis">Battle of Nicopolis which brought about the fall of the Vidin Tsardom in 1396. ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sozopol" title="Sozopol">Sozopol was the last Bulgarian settlement to fall, in 1453.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-63">[60] The Bulgarian nobility was subsequently eliminated and the peasantry was ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serfdom" title="Serfdom">enserfed to Ottoman masters,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Ottoman_rule-62">[59] while much of the educated clergy fled to other countries.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Jire%C4%8Dek-64">[61]

="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians were considered an ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rayah" title="Rayah">inferior class of people under the Ottoman system. Bulgarians were subjected to heavy taxes (including ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devshirme" title="Devshirme">devshirme, or blood tax), their culture was suppressed,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Jire%C4%8Dek-64">[61] and they experienced partial ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamisation">Islamisation.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-65">[62] Ottoman authorities established a religious administrative community called the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rum_Millet" title="Rum Millet">Rum Millet, which governed all Orthodox Christians regardless of their ethnicity.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-66">[63] Most of the local population then gradually lost its distinct national consciousness, identifying only by its faith.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-67">[64]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-68">[65] The clergy remaining in some isolated monasteries kept their ethnic identity alive, enabling its survival in remote rural areas,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrampton19878-69">[66] and in the militant ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_Bulgaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism in Bulgaria">Catholic community in the northwest of the country.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-70">[67]

As Ottoman power began to wane, ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Austria and Russia saw Bulgarian Christians as potential allies. The ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Austrians first backed an ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Tarnovo_Uprising" title="First Tarnovo Uprising">uprising in Tarnovo in 1598, then ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Tarnovo_Uprising" title="Second Tarnovo Uprising">a second one in 1686, the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiprovtsi_Uprising" class="mw-redirect" title="Chiprovtsi Uprising">Chiprovtsi Uprising in 1688 and finally ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karposh%27s_Rebellion" class="mw-redirect" title="Karposh's Rebellion">Karposh's Rebellion in 1689.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Decline-71">[68] The ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire also asserted itself as a protector of Christians in Ottoman lands with the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_K%C3%BC%C3%A7%C3%BCk_Kaynarca" title="Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca">Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca in 1774.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Decline-71">[68]

The Western European ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment" title="Age of Enlightenment">Enlightenment in the 18th century influenced the initiation of a ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_awakening_of_Bulgaria" title="National awakening of Bulgaria">national awakening of Bulgaria.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Ottoman_rule-62">[59] It restored national consciousness and provided an ideological basis for the liberation struggle, resulting in the 1876 ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/April_Uprising" class="mw-redirect" title="April Uprising">April Uprising. Up to 30,000 Bulgarians were killed as Ottoman authorities put down the rebellion. The massacres prompted the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Powers#History" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Powers">Great Powers to take action.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Final_Move_to_Independence-72">[69] They convened the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantinople_Conference" title="Constantinople Conference">Constantinople Conference in 1876, but their decisions were rejected by the Ottomans. This allowed the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire to seek a military solution without risking confrontation with other Great Powers, as had happened in the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">Crimean War.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Final_Move_to_Independence-72">[69] In 1877 ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War,_1877%E2%80%931878" class="mw-redirect" title="Russo-Turkish War, 1877–1878">Russia declared war on the Ottomans and defeated them with the help of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opalchentsi" class="mw-redirect" title="Opalchentsi">Bulgarian rebels, particularly during the crucial ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Shipka_Pass" title="Battle of Shipka Pass">Battle of Shipka Pass which secured Russian control over the main road to ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-73">[70]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-74">[71]

role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Bulgaria_(1878%E2%80%931946)" title="History of Bulgaria (1878–1946)">History of Bulgaria (1878–1946), ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Bulgaria" title="People's Republic of Bulgaria">People's Republic of Bulgaria, and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Bulgaria_since_1989" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Bulgaria since 1989">History of Bulgaria since 1989>

The ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_San_Stefano" title="Treaty of San Stefano">Treaty of San Stefano was signed on 3 March 1878 by ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russia and the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire. It was to set up an autonomous Bulgarian principality spanning ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moesia" title="Moesia">Moesia, ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macedonia_(region)" title="Macedonia (region)">Macedonia and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace, roughly on the territories of the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Bulgarian_Empire" title="Second Bulgarian Empire">Second Bulgarian Empire.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTESan_Stefano,_Berlin_and_Independence-75">[72]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-76">[73] The other ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Powers#History" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Powers">Great Powers immediately rejected the treaty out of fear that such a large country in the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans might threaten their interests. It was superseded by the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Berlin,_1878" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Berlin, 1878">Treaty of Berlin, signed on 13 July, which provided for a much smaller state only comprising Moesia and the region of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia, leaving large populations of ethnic Bulgarians outside the new country.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTESan_Stefano,_Berlin_and_Independence-75">[72]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-BBCProfile-77">[74] This significantly contributed to Bulgaria's militaristic foreign affairs approach during the first half of the 20th century.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHistorical_Setting-78">[75]

The Bulgarian principality won ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbo-Bulgarian_War" title="Serbo-Bulgarian War">a war against Serbia and incorporated the semi-autonomous Ottoman territory of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Rumelia" title="Eastern Rumelia">Eastern Rumelia in 1885, proclaiming itself an independent state on 5 October 1908.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrampton2007174-79">[76] In the years following independence, Bulgaria increasingly militarized and was often referred to as "the Balkan ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia".="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-80">[77] It became involved in three consecutive conflicts between 1912 and 1918—two ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkan_Wars" title="Balkan Wars">Balkan Wars and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I. After a disastrous defeat in the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Balkan_War" title="Second Balkan War">Second Balkan War, Bulgaria again found itself fighting on the losing side as a result of its alliance with the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers in World War I. Despite fielding more than a quarter of its population in a 1,200,000-strong army="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-81">[78]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-82">[79] and achieving several decisive victories at ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Doiran_(1917)" title="Battle of Doiran (1917)">Doiran and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Monastir_(1917)" title="Battle of Monastir (1917)">Monastir, the country capitulated in 1918. The war resulted in significant territorial losses and a total of 87,500 soldiers killed.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-83">[80] More than 253,000 refugees from the lost territories ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immigrated" class="mw-redirect" title="Immigrated">immigrated to Bulgaria from 1912 to 1929,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-84">[81] placing additional strain on the already ruined national economy.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-85">[82]

The resulting political unrest led to the establishment of a royal ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism" title="Authoritarianism">authoritarian dictatorship by Tsar ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boris_III_of_Bulgaria" title="Boris III of Bulgaria">Boris III (1918–1943). Bulgaria entered World War II in 1941 as a member of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axis_Powers" class="mw-redirect" title="Axis Powers">the Axis but declined to participate in ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rescue_of_the_Bulgarian_Jews" title="Rescue of the Bulgarian Jews">saved its Jewish population from deportation to ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_concentration_camps" title="Nazi concentration camps">concentration camps.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBulgaria_in_World_War_II:_The_Passive_Alliance-86">[83] The sudden death of Boris III in mid-1943 pushed the country into political turmoil as the war turned against Germany, and the communist guerrilla movement gained momentum. The government of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bogdan_Filov" title="Bogdan Filov">Bogdan Filov subsequently failed to achieve peace with the Allies. Bulgaria did not comply with Soviet demands to expel German forces from its territory, resulting in a declaration of war and an invasion by the USSR in September 1944.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWartime_Crisis-87">[84] The communist-dominated ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatherland_Front_(Bulgaria)" title="Fatherland Front (Bulgaria)">Fatherland Front took power, ended participation in the Axis and joined the Allied side until the war ended.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-88">[85] Bulgaria suffered little war damage and the Soviet Union demanded no reparations. But all wartime gains, with the notable exception of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Craiova" title="Treaty of Craiova">Southern Dobrudzha, were lost.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Soviet_Occupation-89">[86]

The ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_of_1944" class="mw-redirect" title="Bulgarian coup d'état of 1944">left-wing uprising of 9 September 1944 led to the abolition of the monarchy and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Court_(Bulgaria)" title="People's Court (Bulgaria)">the executions of some 1,000–3,000 dissidents, war criminals, and members of the former royal elite.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-90">[87]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-91">[88]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-92">[89] But it was not until 1946 that a ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-party_state" title="One-party state">one-party ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_republic" class="mw-redirect" title="People's republic">people's republic was instituted following a referendum.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrampton2005271-93">[90] It fell into the Soviet sphere of influence under the leadership of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgi_Dimitrov" title="Georgi Dimitrov">Georgi Dimitrov (1946–1949), who established a repressive, rapidly industrializing ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stalinism" title="Stalinism">Stalinist state.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Soviet_Occupation-89">[86] By the mid-1950s standards of living rose significantly and political repressions eased.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDomestic_Policy_and_Its_Results-94">[91]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAfter_Stalin-95">[92] The Soviet-style ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planned_economy" title="Planned economy">planned economy saw some market-oriented policies emerging on an experimental level under ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todor_Zhivkov" title="Todor Zhivkov">Todor Zhivkov (1954–1989).="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Economy-96">[93] Compared to wartime levels, national GDP increased five-fold and per capita GDP quadrupled by the 1980s,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-97">[94] although severe debt spikes took place in 1960, 1977 and 1980.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-98">[95] Zhivkov's daughter ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyudmila_Zhivkova" title="Lyudmila Zhivkova">Lyudmila bolstered national pride by promoting Bulgarian heritage, culture and arts worldwide.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Political_Atmosphere_in_the_1970s-99">[96] Facing declining birth rates among the ethnic Bulgarian majority, in 1984 Zhivkov's government forced the minority ethnic ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turks_in_Bulgaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Turks in Bulgaria">Turks to adopt Slavic names in an attempt to erase their identity and assimilate them.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBulgaria_in_the_1980s-100">[97] These policies resulted in the emigration of some 300,000 ethnic Turks to Turkey.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-101">[98]="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-102">[99]

The Communist Party was forced to give up its political monopoly on 10 November 1989 under the influence of the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolutions_of_1989" title="Revolutions of 1989">Revolutions of 1989. Zhivkov resigned and Bulgaria embarked on a transition to a parliamentary democracy.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGovernment_and_Politics-103">[100] The first free elections in June 1990 were won by the Communist Party, now rebranded as the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarian_Socialist_Party" title="Bulgarian Socialist Party">Bulgarian Socialist Party.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-104">[101] A ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Bulgaria" title="Constitution of Bulgaria">new constitution that provided for a relatively weak elected president and for a prime minister accountable to the legislature was adopted in July 1991. The new system initially failed to improve living standards or create economic growth—the average quality of life and economic performance remained lower than under communism well into the early 2000s.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-105">[102] After 2001 economic, political and geopolitical conditions improved greatly,="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTELibrary_of_Congress200616-106">[103] and Bulgaria achieved high Human Development status in 2003.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-107">[104] It became a member of ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO in 2004="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-nato-108">[105] and participated in the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">War in Afghanistan. After several years of reforms it joined the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Single_Market" title="European Single Market">single market in 2007 despite Brussels' concerns about government corruption.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-Ind-109">[106] Bulgaria hosted the 2018 ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidency_of_the_Council_of_the_European_Union" title="Presidency of the Council of the European Union">Presidency of the Council of the European Union at the National Palace of Culture in Sofia.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-110">[107]

Bulgaria occupies a portion of the eastern ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkan peninsula, bordering five countries—="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey to the south, ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macedonia_(country)" class="mw-redirect" title="Macedonia (country)">Macedonia and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia to the west, and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania to the north. Bulgaria has a changeable climate, which results from being positioned at the meeting point of the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean and continental air masses combined with the barrier effect of its mountains.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTELibrary_of_Congress20064-111">[108] Northern Bulgaria averages 1 °C (1.8 °F) cooler, and registers 200 millimetres (7.9 in) more precipitation, than the regions south of the Balkan mountains. Bulgaria is a ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentary democracy">parliamentary democracy where the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Bulgaria" title="Prime Minister of Bulgaria">prime minister is the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government and the most powerful executive position.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTELibrary_of_Congress200616-106">[103] The political system has three branches—legislative, executive and judicial, with ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_suffrage" title="Universal suffrage">universal suffrage for ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Citizen">citizens at least 18 years old. Unlike the prime minister, presidential domestic power is more limited. The directly elected ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Bulgaria" title="President of Bulgaria">president serves as ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state and ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commander-in-chief" title="Commander-in-chief">commander-in-chief of the armed forces, and has the authority to return a bill for further debate, although the parliament can override the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_veto" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidential veto">presidential veto by a simple majority vote.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTELibrary_of_Congress200616-106">[103] Political parties gather in the ="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_(Bulgaria)" title="National Assembly (Bulgaria)">National Assembly, a body of 240 deputies elected to four-year terms by direct popular vote. The National Assembly has the power to enact laws, approve the budget, schedule presidential elections, select and dismiss the prime minister and other ministers, declare war, deploy troops abroad, and ratify international treaties and agreements.="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria#cite_note-FOOTNOTELibrary_of_Congress200616%E2%80%9317-142">[139]
